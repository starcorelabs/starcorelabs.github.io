Reverse Job Description

2021-06-17

Hello,

This post is specifically directed to technical recruiters who send me unsolicited job descriptions on a regular basis. Most are for positions outside my field of UX Design.

I’ve created this “Reverse Job Description” so recruiters will know what I do and what I might be open to.

Currently I’m working for a good company and I’m not looking for a new job.
If you're a recruiter, keep reading to understand what it would take to change my mind.

Summary

Acceptable Titles: UX/UI Designer, Visual Designer, Graphic Designer.
Location: 100% Remote. Not open to onsite work.
Duration: Full-Time Position.
Salary Range: $85k to $100K.
Preferred Industries: eCommerce, Payment Processing, Renewable Energy, Technology Repair and Recycling.

Company

  • Values people over profit.
  • Flat organizational structure where everyone is part of one team.
  • Data-driven and customer centered.
  • Profitable while helping society and protecting the environment.
  • Has a 5 year plan based on customer comments and real world analytics. Adapts the plan to changing circumstances.
  • Projects are organised into milestone goals and broken down into individual tasks per employee.
  • Sells products and services by value not hourly rate.
  • Realistically plans projects by including time for expected delays from employee's life situations, decision by committee, iterative design, errors, and refactoring.
  • Open to ideas and suggestions from staff on how to improve efficient operations to save time and money. Even if it requires changing corporate policy.
  • Security doesn't block require software and online resources necessary to do the job.  

Management

  • Focused on tasks that produce measurable results.
  • Decision making is based on verified evidence from marketing analytics and focus groups.
  • Transparent about company policies and internal politics.
  • Has empathy for employees.
  • Solid time management skills without rushing themselves or others.
  • Clear realistic expectations and goals in writing.
  • Open door policy. With the ability to accept different opinions, ideas, and criticism.
  • Gets to know individuals on the team including moods, likes and dislikes.
  • Understands the value each individual brings to their job.
  • Doesn’t waste time in meetings when an email will do.  

Role

  • UX/UI Design, Visual Designer, Graphic Designer
  • Focused on Visual Design of user interfaces for web apps
  • Able to create graphical assets for developers
  • User Flows and Journeys
  • Mockups
  • Prototypes
  • Understands the limits of HTML and CSS but never required to write code
  • Focused on using Adobe XD for Mockups and Prototypes.
  • Minor use of Photoshop and Illustrator for graphical assets.  

Compensation

  • $85k to $100K salary
  • Flexible Schedule
  • Life Insurance
  • Medical
  • Dental
  • Vision
  • Optional 401k or Roth
  • Unlimited PTO
  • Vacation time that never expires  

Location

  • 100% Remote with no requirement to work onsite or attend in-person events.  

Deal Breakers

  • Prioritizing company policy and profit over people.
  • Required to work onsite in an office.
  • Hostile work environments and toxic employees.
  • Security that locks up employee computers and makes it difficult or impossible to work.
  • Forced to sit through pointless time wasting meetings.
  • Using corporate jargon, processes, and vague language to avoid clear communication.
  • Time Boxing, which includes Sprints.
  • Fast paced environments.
  • Once per month paycheck.
  • Two or more jobs under one title.
  • Adding extra duties to a predefined and agreed upon role.
  • Unrealistic expectations based on personal preferences and/or lack of knowledge.
  • Selling services on an hourly basis instead of by value.
  • Assuming busy means productive and successful.
  • Stringing employees along with verbal promises of advancement while refusing to put anything in writing.  

What I Bring To The Table

Experience

I have 8 years of experience in UX/UI Design working across large projects in corporate environments.

Professional Skills

  • Adapt to change quickly
  • Interpersonal skills
  • A team player but can work independently
  • Solid written and verbal communication
  • Time management
  • Mood control (stay calm under pressure)
  • Critical thinking
  • Positive “Can Do” attitude
  • Creative, proactive, and solution-focused
  • Growth mindset, always learning
  • Computer literacy with Windows, Mac, and Linux
  • Microsoft Office
  • Internet research skills
  • Familiar with Waterfall and Agile style project management  

Design Skills

  • Graphic Design Fundamentals
  • UX/UI Design Fundamentals
  • Mobile First Design Principles
  • Understanding Responsive Design
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Adobe XD
  • Figma
  • Wireframes
  • Mockups
  • Prototypes
  • Designing with the limitations of HTML and CSS in mind
  • Advocating for the end user
