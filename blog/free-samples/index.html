Online Privacy

Here's my suggestions for staying safe online.

  1. Install your preferred Linux OS on whatever device you want to use as your daily driver.

Here's 3 lightweight easy to use Linux Distros to choose from.

  • Lubuntu
  • Linux Mint
  • POP OS  This is my favorite

Here's a YouTube video that will show you step-by-step how to set up the latest POP OS.

  1. Use a VPN like Private Internet Access
    Its cheap and easy to use. Plus they are the only ones who've proven in court that they do not log your activity.

  2. Use Brave Browser with the following plugins

    • DuckDuckGo Privacy Essentials
    • Adblock Plus
    • HTTPS Everywhere
    • Privacy Badger

  1. Use DuckDuckGo as your search engine. They don't track you.

  2. Update your passwords

    • Use different passwords for every site you access
    • Use Password Meter to test the strength of your password before you use it.
    • Use LastPass to manage all your passwords for you. They even have a secure password generator built-in.
    • Or use this Secure Password Generator with a minimum of 32 characters.

  1. Beware of Scammers and Hackers
    • Don't share any personal information with strangers either online, over the phone, or in person.
    • Don't trust a stranger who's in a rush to get your information and/or demands that you pay them.
    • Don't click on links in emails from unknown sources.
    • Don't trust random strangers on social media or other websites.
    • Don't download and install apps or games from unknown or questionable sources.
