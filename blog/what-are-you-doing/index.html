What Are You Doing?

2024-04-28

Sometimes family and friends ask me what I'm doing.

I know this is a bait question from years of experience, and they don't care what I'm doing.
They're looking for someone or something to entertain them.

I would tell them they are barking up the wrong tree, but I know they won't listen.

This is one of the many reasons I've implemented a "Zero Trust, Zero Tolerance" policy.

Here are some of the other reasons why they ask what I'm doing:

  • They are bored and have nothing better to do.
  • They're prepared to misunderstand my reply and start a conflict.
  • They want to challenge me in some way to feel dominant, superior, and powerful.
  • They are frustrated about something and want to complain to me.
  • They want to rope me into a debate on a topic I don't care about.
  • They feel an irrational need to compare their level of success against mine. Attempting to create a false competition where none exists.
  • They want me to do something for them, usually at my expense.

Going forward, I refuse to play any of those games.

No one contacts me to ask how I'm doing. Nor do they offer to help me with my projects or problems.

Not because they are bad people. But because they are thinking about themselves, their feelings, and what they want first.

My usual answer to their question is, "Nothing new. Just watching YouTube".

But, I don't go into detail because I know they don't want to hear that I'm also:

  • Cooking
  • Cleaning
  • Taking out the trash
  • Reading books
  • Watching Movies and TV Shows
  • Taking courses to improve my knowledge and skills
  • Working on projects
  • Playing old video games

Those activities don't relieve their boredom or make them feel excited or in control.

I've realized I'm better off focusing on improving myself and avoiding drama. That means stepping back from people who will not or cannot, help me accomplish my goals.

I don't have anything against anyone. I'm minding my own business and doing what works for me.
