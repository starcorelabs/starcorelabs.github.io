Free Resources for Learning Graphic Design

You do not have to go to college or design school to be a graphic designer.

I'll say it again. Most clients and employers do not care if you have a degree from a fancy school. All they care about is if you have the skills and you can do the work.

All a degree shows, is that you can follow rules and pass tests. What it doesn't show is that you can manage your time while creatively solving problems. Can you deal with co-workers and clients effectively while still getting the work done? How can you show potential clients and employers that they should hire you?

The answer is you must have an online portfolio of work. Along with a decent resume.

Of course if you're new to Graphic Design you won't have any client work to post. This means you'll need to create a dozen or more Logos, Icons, Infographics, and Website mockups.

You can pick existing items and redesign them or use your imagination and come up with your own designs. Either way, you'll want to show that you have a range of skills through your portfolio.

Below is a set of resources that will help you develop your skills.

YOUTUBE VIDEOS

I love learning from watching videos. Some of the "How-To" videos on YouTube rival those of college and paid for online training courses. You'll have to invest some time searching through YouTube to find the diamonds in the rough. I've complied a short list of my favorites to get you started.

TastyTuts Creative tutorials by Gareth David

Gareth David is a Freelance Graphic Designer who posts amazingly professional Graphic Design related videos. His beginner's courses are, in my option, the best beginning videos online. You should subscribe to his channel.

Roberto Blake

Roberto Blake is another YouTube Graphic Designer who focuses on providing information for other freelancers and entrepreneurs. His videos are a bit more advanced. So you'll want to ground yourself in the basics of Graphic Design first.

  • How to Become a Graphic Designer
  • 5 Things You Don't Learn in Graphic Design School

Will Paterson

Will is a great YouTube personality who explains Graphic Design related topics in easy to understand ways.

  • How To Get Good At Graphic Design

Websites

There are some great websites that will give you free information and resources to help you learn the skills of being a Graphic Designer. Keep in mind you'll want to check post dates to make sure the information you're getting is as updated as possible. Anything posted within the last 3 years is going to be relevant and helpful. Older posts and even historical texts about Graphic Design will help you develop a better foundation of knowledge.

In my opinion, developing practical marketable skills should come first. If you are trying to get a job working for a company. Spend your time wisely learning what employer's in your area are looking for. Then focus on learning and/or improving those skills as quickly as possible. If you plan on only freelancing this step will still be helpful. You'll learn what skills are the most in demand in your area.

You can start your research by going to the top employment sites, Dice, Monster, CareerBuilder, and LinkedIn. Search for Graphic Design Jobs in your area. Take notes on the what the employers want you to know. Of course you'll need to know Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign. Those are the industry standard software all Graphic Designers should know how to use.

What you'll need to be aware of is what they want you to do and what restrictions you'll be working under. What will your daily workload look like? What other skills, both Graphic Design and non-Graphic Design, will they expect from you? Will you be given time to work on one project at a time or will you be expected to multitask? Will you have the chance to focus and flow or will there be constant interruptions? This is where picking the right company culture comes into play.

Tutsplus.com - a website devoted to How-To Tutorials, eBooks and paid courses.

They post some very good articles on all kinds of topics. The older link below is a how-to article that mostly stands up as foundational knowledge for Graphic Designers. Beware, some of the links in the article are dead. Teach Yourself Graphic Design: A Self-Study Course Outline.

Hubspot.com - A marketing focused site that also hosts articles around Design and Development related topics. Keep in mind this is very marketing centric so it might not fit what you are looking for.

Want to Teach Yourself Design? 8 Tips & Tricks for Beginners

Canva.com - A web application that lets you design in the browser. Not as robust as Photoshop or Illustrator but the tool can produce some interesting designs quickly. Especially if you don't have access to Adobe Suite or the online CC version.

The article below is interesting for Designers who want to keep up with trends. The 9 Graphic Design Trends You Need to Be Aware of In 2016

Skilled Up - An online training site for people wanting to improve their skills. This older article covers more courses that might be helpful to beginning Graphic Designers. I haven't had the time to test all the links or look at all the courses. The few I looked at seemed decent.

14 Best Online Graphic Design Courses for Beginners

Codecademy - A free online training site that helps you learn coding. The course below is for the basics of HTML & CSS. Something all Graphic Designers should know. Learn HTML & CSS

Meetup.com - This site helps people of like interests meet and share their passions.

You can search for Graphic Design groups in your area. If you don't find any, or any you like. You have the option to start or own group.

BOOKS

You can Google "Top Books for Graphic Designers" and you'll get a huge list of books to look at.

Here's a few sites I found with lists I liked. I've only read a handful of these books. Clearly the more you read the better. You'll notice some of the books on these lists are repeated. Those are the key books to read.

  • The 10 best graphic design books of 2015
  • 10 Graphic Design Books Every Designer Should Read
  • 10 essential books every graphic designer must read
  • 25 of the Best Design Books of 2015

As you can see the topic of Graphic Design is huge. There are so many different types of design with different styles and perspectives. It really can get overwhelming if you get too caught up in trying to amass all the information before you start.

The information above will keep you busy for a long time. Pick one video, site, or book and work through it completely before moving on to the next thing. Your best option is to look at the YouTube Beginner's guide to Graphic Design by Gareth David. He covers all of this, in simple to understand short videos. Along with links for further reading.
