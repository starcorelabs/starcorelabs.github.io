Free Tools for Graphic Designers

If you are reading this chances are you're a beginning Graphic Designer. You might want to learn the three essential tools for Graphic Design: Photoshop, Illustrator and InDesign. But you've found that Adobe has moved to a monthly subscription based payment model. Which is a bit too expensive for you, as you are still learning the basics.

Thankfully there are free open source programs that share similar functionally to Adobe's applications.

Even though these applications are free and open source they are quite complex and powerful.

There are a lot of Graphic Designers who use them professionally. Keep in mind they are not clones of Adobe's software so while they share some core functionally they solve design problems in different ways.

The skills you learn from these three free programs will transfer over to Adobe's applications when you can afford to switch. You'll still have to learn the Adobe applications interfaces and workflow. But your learning curve will be easier.

You'll find, with a couple of online tutorials, these programs are easy and fun to use.

Photoshop Equivalent GIMP

GIMP is a cross-platform image editor available for GNU/Linux, OS X, Windows and more operating systems.

YouTube GIMP Tutorial for Beginners

Illustrator Equivalent Inkscape

Inkscape is an open-source vector graphics editor similar to Adobe Illustrator, Corel Draw, or Freehand.

YouTube Inkscape Tutorial

InDesign Equivalent Scribus

Scribus is a cross platform open-source page layout program.

YouTube Scribus Tutorial
