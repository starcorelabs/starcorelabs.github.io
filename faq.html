Frequently Asked Questions

As an Information Technology Professional I've trained myself to look for patterns.

Over the last year I've spoken with over a hundred recuiters and noticed the same repeated questions. I decided to create this F.A.Q. to save time and effort.

If you have any questions not on the list below, please email me and I'll answer them.

  • Recruiter: How are you?
  • Michael: I’m great thanks for asking.
  • Recruiter: What city are you in? Are you willing to relocate?
  • Michael: I live in Indianapolis. I am willing to relocate if the role is full time with benefits and the company pays for relocation.
  • Recruiter: Tell me about your background?
  • Michael: Back in 2004 I taught myself Photoshop. I used it to create graphics for local bands. Not long after that I taught myself HTML and CSS which I used to create static websites. In 2007 I went to a vocational school to learn how to build and repair computers. After graduating I worked as a tech support agent in call centers for a few years while freelancing on the side. I got tired of working in tech support and decided to jump the fence and move into design as my main profession.
  • Recruiter: What is your career focus?
  • Michael: My goal is to find a UX Design role where I can create wireframes and prototypes for web applications. I've had some Front-End Development roles in the past. They were Bootstrap and CSS/SASS focused.
  • Recruiter: Where did you learn your skills?
  • Michael: I’m entirely self taught. I learned from books, online video courses, hands-on experiments, and while working in corporate environments.
  • Recruiter: What design software do you use?
  • Michael: Photoshop, Illustrator, XD, Balsamiq, Gimp, Inkscape, Figma, and sometimes I'll sketch designs on paper.
  • Recruiter: Was your last job contract or direct hire?
  • Michael: My last few jobs were contract due to what was available at the time.
  • Recruiter: Why did you leave your last position(s)?
  • Michael: The contract ended.
  • Recruiter: What did you do in your last role?
  • Michael: I was part of a 3 person UX team that was tasked with fixing code errors in front-end pages for a C# MVC web application. We compared the pages that were built by the offshore team against prototypes to find errors. We rewrote Bootstrap HTML and CSS/SASS code to correct where the components were placed on pages. Testing with software, and visually, to make sure the pages worked correctly on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.
  • Recruiter: What freelance projects have you worked on?
  • Michael: I've designed logos, created cover art for bands, made graphics for marketing and social media. As well as setup and customized Wordpress websites for clients.
  • Recruiter: Your last few roles were in development but you call yourself a designer?
  • Michael: Yes, I took what was available. Most of my past roles were a mix of front-end development with some design.
  • Recruiter: Are you opposed to doing development?
  • Michael: My focus is on UX Design. But, I am open to doing Front-End Development. Specifically HTML, CSS/SCSS, Bootstrap, JavaScript, and jQuery.
  • Recruiter: Have you used other responsive frameworks besides Bootstrap?
  • Michael: I am more experienced with Bootstrap. A few years ago while working in a corporate job I used Skeleton for a project. Another company I worked for considered using Foundation but decided to go with Bootstrap because that's what all the developers were familiar with.
  • Recruiter: Do you know any JavaScript frameworks? Examples: React, Angular, Vue, Backbone, Underscore, or Something Else
  • Michael: Short answer: No, I've not had the opportunity to use them.
    Long answer: The corporate enviroments I've worked in were Microsoft shops. Projects were written in C# MVC for the backend. I wrote HTML, CSS/SCSS, Bootstrap, and Razor for views. We used Node and Gulp for automation of tasks on the front-end.
  • Recruiter: Have you done any native app development for Android or iPhone?
  • Michael: No, its all front-end web development experience.
  • Recruiter: What is your strongest development skill?
  • Michael: I'm great with CSS/SASS. Its like designing with code.
  • Recruiter: What about Back-End Programming? For example: C#, .Net, PHP, Python, Ruby, or Java.
  • Michael: I've worked as a Front-End Developer on a team supporting C# programmers. But I do not know C# or back-end programming. Learning to program is like learning to speak a foreign language. It takes a lot of time and practice. I've spent my time learning design.
  • Recruiter: Have you written code for Databases? Exampes: SQL, MySQL, or Oracle
  • Michael: No, my experience has been on design and front-end developement. Databases are on the opposite end of the spectrum from my skill set.
  • Recruiter: Your resume seems short. Is this it, or is there more?
  • Michael: You can see my full employment history on my LinkedIn profile. I’ve focused my resume on my design skills and kept it simple to fit on one page.
  • Recruiter: Looking at your resume your last job was some time ago. Have you been looking?
  • Michael: I am actively looking for a job. I’m confident the right role is out there if I keep looking.
  • Recruiter: So are you available immediately?
  • Michael: Yes, I am available immediately.
  • Recruiter: What platform are you most comfortable with Mac or Windows?
  • Michael: I am familiar with Windows, Mac, and Linux. But, I prefer Windows.
  • Recruiter: What is your target salary?
  • Michael: Depending on the requirements of the position my range is $60K to $75K.
  • Recruiter: Thank you for your time. Hope you have a good day.
  • Michael: Thanks. Have a great day!
