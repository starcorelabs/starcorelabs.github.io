Burn Out


Disclaimer: All views expressed are my personal opinions and feelings. They do not reflect on any specific people or organizations. Read at your own risk!

This post will be different from everything I’ve written previously. Up until now I have avoided talking about myself. My focus has been to write positive valuable content for my readers.

Crispy

I am burnt out on writing code and web development. I have all the classic symptoms:

  • Exhaustion and fatigue
  • Lack of motivation
  • Frustration, cynicism, negativity, anger and irritability
  • Self criticism (I beat myself up horribly all the time)
  • Helplessness and futility
  • Cognitive problems (trouble focusing on tasks and remembering details)
  • Slipping job performance
  • Interpersonal problems at work
  • Not taking care of one’s self
  • Being preoccupied with work, when not at work
  • Health Problems (I get migraine headaches often)

Some people live by the philosophy, “As long as the company pays me, I don’t care what I do.” Their agenda is to earn as much money as possible. In contrast, my philosophy, life is made of time. A precious non-renewable resource.

Right now I trade my skills and time for a paycheck. But, my job is not my life. It is simply a set of tasks I preform to earn money. Yes money is important, but in my opinion mental health is infinitely more valuable.

Considering how much time I spend at work. I need to enjoy what I’m doing and who I work with. I’m constantly thinking of all the time I will never get back. I quietly ask myself, “Is this how I want to spend my time?”

Even if the people I work with are awesome. Doing tasks I do not enjoy simply for a paycheck eats at me. I feel like a cog in a machine. Depression sets in and I feel as if I’ve been stabbed in the gut with a flaming dagger. I lose the ability to focus on tasks or remember previous meetings. It all blurs together into a haze of frustration and dread. Overtime I become apathetic.

Sick and tired of…

  • Having no control over my time or assigned tasks.
  • Power games co-workers play in an attempt to make me look bad while trying to impress managment and climb the corporate ladder.
  • Being expected to figure out the ever-changing office politics and bureaucratic procedures of my job.
  • Trying to stay current on the moving target of the latest and greatest coding standards and languages.
  • Vague tasks that always require clarification. I’m expected to contact one of ten people for input and/or permission to make a change. Waiting for them to meet with their colleagues and/or supervior before getting back to me. This makes a two minute task take two days to complete. Feels like trying to ice skate up hill.
  • The endless stream of meaningless tasks that rises up, threatening to crush me like a tsunami. Most of which is busy work designed to validate someone’s job or appease bureaucratic requirements.
  • Bureaucracy and pointless meetings. Decisions made during meetings change an hour later. Why am I’m expected to mind-read people I never talk with to know things changed? While, at the same time, write perfect code for every task?
  • Conflicting with other people who unconsciously ascribe to a consumer culture of self-absorbed competition and petty judgment. Who become hostile when I don’t kowtow and agree with their worldview.

Goal

It would be amazing to run my own small business providing value to my customers. I am motivated by my creativity to research problems, create solutions, and write them out.

There are so many things I could be doing with my time that might not pay as much. Yet, provide me with the sense of fulfillment I’m lacking. I am currently researching alternative careers and entrepreneurship for inspiration. Hopefully soon I’ll have a solution to my situation.
