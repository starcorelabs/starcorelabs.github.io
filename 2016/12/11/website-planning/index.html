Website Planning

If you fail to plan, you’re planning to fail.

Before you build your next website stop and plan it out in detail. Clearly you’ll want as much traffic as possible for your site. So spend the time upfront crafting it to give you the results you want.

Regardless if you are building a blog, a sales site, or a web application your entry point should always be from your users perspective.

Who are your visitors? What demographic are you targeting? Age, gender, education, income, profession, interests, and any other factors that are relevant to your website.

From this emerging profile you can figure out what you’re visitors problems might be.
Pick a niche you are interested in and know something about.

For example if you are building a blog to serve bakers. Do your homework and search online for bakers problems. Find out what their top 10 complaints about their industry and daily work are. This will give you the entry point to creating posts that address their issues.

If you plan to sell products and/or services to your audience. Knowing their issues will give you a starting point on what kinds of products you can build that will solve their problems.

Start with a small inexpensive ebook they can download that addresses most of your visitors issues and costs $5.00 or less. Promote this on your site and social media with teasers about the solutions you’ve researched and complied together. Make it easy for your customers to join your mailing list and buy your ebook. Keep things simple.

How will your users find your site? This is where SEO and Social Marketing comes into play. Do some research on Google and YouTube to learn more about SEO. When your target audience looks online for solutions to their problems. You want your site on the first page of a Google Search. SEO or Search Engine Optimization should be built into the foundation of your website. As well as invested in every post you write.

You’ll need to secure a strong social media marketing effort on Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube if you want to drive traffic your website. Again, reseach Social Media Marketing online for tutorials that will show you how to do this.
