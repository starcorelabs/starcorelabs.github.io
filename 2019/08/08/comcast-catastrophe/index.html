Comcast Catastrophe

2019-08-08

Since Friday 08/02 I’ve been trying to get Comcast cable Internet at my new apartment. Its been a frustrating battle to say the least.

Back in 2013 at my old apartment it took 10 minutes to connect the equipment and call the company. After a quick test I was online. Based on this past experience I assumed setting it up at my new apartment would be quick and easy. No such luck.

Because I’m at work most of the time I only want basic Internet at my apartment. Here’s the plan that fit what I was looking for.

Its $20 a month for 25Mbps down. Notice this offer says FREE Self-Install kit. That’s a total lie.

When I called them and ask them to turn on the service they lied to me and told me that my new apartment is only provisioned for cable TV. I spoke with my apartment office staff and they said, “Many residents have Comcast Internet”.

Then they lied to me again and told me they’d have to send up a special request ticket to get it turned on at my location. They said it could take up to 6 days and then I’d have to pay $70 for a tech to come out and connect a coaxial cable.

What they don’t know is I was tech support agent for 7 years before becoming a UI Developer. My modem is hooked up and I see a green online ready light which tells me the cable is already hooked up. I know all they have to do is get my modem’s mac address and add it to a speed tier in their system.

But instead of making it simple they over-complicate it to try and confuse me into paying more than it’s worth.

Now they require a year contract with paperless billing and autopay. Which means I don’t get any documentation to show what I’m paying for and they can raise my rate anytime they want. Which they’ve tried to do to me in the past when I wasn’t under a contract. If I turn off autopay they will charge me an extra $10 a month. If I cancel the service before the contract ends I’ll owe them an early termination fee of $500. Plus, I was informed that I’d have to give them a $50.00 deposit before activation.

But this doesn’t included all the insane rules associated with their service.

Regardless, let’s do math to find out how much it would cost me for this FREE Self-Install:

  • $50.00 deposit
  • $70.00 install fee
  • $20.00 for the first month
  • 140.00 total

Is this what business is about these days? Lying and trying to confuse people to make it easier to scam them.

Whatever happened to providing a quality service at a reasonable price?

What happened to earning loyalty by making customers happy?

Seems like all they care about is tricking people into agreeing to a huge monthly fee, with complex rules they can change whenever they like, regardless of what you want or need.

After 6 days of them stringing me along, listening to their lies, I got tired of the game. I told them I refuse to pay a $70 install fee for a service they claimed on their site was “Free Self-Install”. I know its a scam. I told them I’ll go without Internet at my apartment if I have to.

They wanted me to think I don’t have a choice. But I know I do because I have Internet access at my job. Due to their shady practices they’ve lost a customer for life. After all that I’ll never use them again.

I’m currently researching other options in my area. I won’t agree to anything until I do the math and make sure its a good deal.
