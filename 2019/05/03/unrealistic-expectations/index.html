Unrealistic Expectations

2019-05-03

In all aspects of life Unrealistic Expectations set you up for frustration and failure. This is usually self-imposed suffering.

Here’s the guidelines I follow to avoid creating or allowing Unrealistic Expectations in my life:

  • Focus on your own goals and tasks.
  • You are the sum of the 5 people you spend the most time with. Be careful whom you allow around you. Their behavior will affect you.
  • Don’t expect other people to think or act the way you want them to. Either they share your values or they don’t. Keep doing what works for you.
  • Most people act in what they feel is their own best interest. Regardless of how it might affect you.
  • At work do not accept unrealistic expectations placed on you. Either in the job description when applying or later when the role inevitably evolves.
  • Clearly define your self, your boundaries, and the role you play in personal and business affairs. Do not allow others do define your identity or role.
  • Write down your own expectations and the expectations of others. Then challenge and renegotiate the unrealistic parts. Leave nothing unspoken.
  • Spend your time wisely doing things that benefit you. Like improving your health, cleaning, or sharpening your skills.
  • Don’t allow laziness, excuses, complaints, entertainment, or other people to distract you. Keep your eyes on your goals.

Discipline and success are habits built up consistently over time.
