Old Dell Desktop

2019-06-24

About 8 years ago I gave my Dad and old 32-bit 3GHz Dell Dimension 3000 with 2GB of ram running Windows XP. I set it up so he could play PC games like Diablo 2 and Age of Empires.

It worked well enough for his needs at the time. Unfortunately my Dad is a smoker. He sat in front of the old Dell for hours smoking and playing games. The tar and ash caked the insides of the computer which caused it to heat up and slow down.

A few months ago I rebuilt a newer HP Desktop computer with Windows 7 for him. It is a faster 64 bit computer with 4GB of ram. I copied over all his files and games. He’s been happy with it so far.

I brought the old 32-bit Dell home and left it in a corner not having any use for it. Or, wanting to deal with the nasty smelly mess inside. It sat there for a couple of weeks.

Yesterday I decided it was time to do something with the old fossil. I spent 5 hours cleaning. I took all the components out of the case. Then carefully cleaned each component with compressed air, isopropyl alcohol, q-tips, and tons of paper towels.

Even on surfaces that looked clean the towels turned brown and black. After hours of meticulous cleaning I put most of the computer back together.

All except for the power supply. It was way too dirty and I was unwilling to risk an electric shock attempting to open it up and clean it. Instead I decided to replace it with an old but cleaner power supply I had.

When I booted the system I found it had a bios password. I had to pull the password jumper off the board to reset it. Once it was cleared I tested 5 old IDE drives until I found a 200GB one that still works.

Once I had the hard drive installed put in my 8GB USB drive with 32-bit MX Linux on it. I choose MX Linux because its Debian-based, uses XFCE desktop, and is fast and light.

MX Linux is great. Easy to use, fast, simple, and looks good. It installed quickly without issues. I was surprised how it instantly enabled the old USB Linksys WiFi adapter that I added.

When this desktop was running under Windows XP it was slow. Now with MX Linux its fast. Even after all the cleaning it still has a dusty smoke smell when its running. Regardless, I’m happy with this build.

Still not sure what I’m going to use it for, I’ll keep you posted if I think of anything.
