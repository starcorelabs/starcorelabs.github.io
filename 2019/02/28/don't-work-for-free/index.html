Don't Work For Free

2019-02-28

As a freelancer some people are going to try to take advantage of you. Assuming because you’re not busy working a “real” job that you should give them free suggestions, ideas, research, troubleshooting, designs, or code for their project.

Even if you work full-time and freelance on the side.
Some people are cheap and want “Something for Nothing”.

When people ask me directly or indirectly for free work I take it as a compliment. Because they are telling me they value my skills and creativity.

I ask them to email me a detailed overview of their specific project. Then after considering all the requirements for their project I’ll provide them with a quote.

I will begin working on their project after we’ve agreed on the details, they’ve signed my contract, and sent the first half of the payment.

I do this to protect myself from having my time wasted or being used. This also helps serious clients hammer out the specifics of what they want done on their project.

I understand some clients are price conscious and want to look for the lowest rate possible. I like to remind potential clients:

“The bitterness of poor quality will last long after the sweetness of low price has passed”.

This blog is the only place I give away free content to my readers. I don’t ask for an email address in exchange. I feel if you value what I have to say and like my work you’ll hire me. Otherwise, enjoy my posts.

Tire kickers and cheapskates can keep walking because I have no time for them. In my experience they won’t respect me or value my work even if they get it for free. So I refuse to work with them.
