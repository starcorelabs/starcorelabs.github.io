Adventures in Job Hunting

Its true that job hunting is a job in itself.

After my last job ended at the end of June 2017 I spent months applying for UX Design and Front-End Development roles.

When talking with recruiters or hiring managers I always try to be humble and grateful.

Making sure to tell them I’m adaptable, excited to learn new things, and willing to do things the company’s way.

Over the last year I’ve had a lot of phone calls with recruiters that followed a familiar pattern. I provide them my resume and a brief overview of my work history. They tell me they will talk with their “Account Manager” or someone above them.

Then I wait days or sometimes weeks to hear back from them. If they don’t ghost on me.

For the last year its been all rejections. After so many, they start being funny.
My favorite was when I was told, “You don’t know enough and you’re over-qualified”. I can read between the lines.

As a professional all I need to hear is either, “The company has went with another option”. Or, “Welcome aboard”.

Regardless of past setbacks I’m hopeful I’ll find a good job soon.

Writing

I’ve been improving my writing skills by blogging and reading:

  • On Writing Well by William Zinsser
  • Guide to Good Writing by Martin H. Manser

I was contemplating writing a short eBook. When a friend, who is writing a non-fiction book, asked me to proof-read his and offer suggestions. He hasn’t finished it yet so I cannot say anything specifically about his book.

Generally speaking my suggestions where aimed at improving the flow of his writing and his approach.

He was starting in the middle of the topic using too much jargon. This assumed “niche specific” knowledge on the part of the reader. The average person would be lost reading the first chapter.

I recommended he build up to that in-depth topic by providing more background information. Giving his reader broad strokes to bring them up to speed.

Along with suggestions on a chronological order of events and topics to smooth out the experience for his readers.

Since then he’s reported that my suggestions have altered the direction of his book. Opening up options he hadn’t thought of. Overall improving his book’s examination of its subject matter.
