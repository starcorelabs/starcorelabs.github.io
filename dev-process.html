Web Development

Development Process

This is a detailed description of the process I go through for every client.

Before we get into the process I want to be clear about the things I don't do.

Out of Scope

  • Graphics and Animation
  • Web copy
  • Marketing
  • Music and/or Video Production
  • Domain Registration and/or Hosting
  • Administration or management of other people’s web sites

Note: All of these things are handled by other companies and/or freelancers. I can recommend a few if you like.

Clients

I offer my services to anyone who wants a web site. Weather you are an individual who wants to start a blog or a small business that needs to build a site. Or maybe you want to move your existing website to Wordpress to make it easier to use. I can help with that.

Development Process Outline

Here’s the steps I go though on each project.

  1. Skype Meeting
  2. Website Types
  3. Service Packages
  4. Scope
  5. Contract
  6. Resources
  7. Development Build
  8. Payment
  9. Transfer
  10. Remove Access

1. Skype Meeting

I prefer to have a Skype meeting to get to know my client’s business and goals.

I encourage them to think of their website as a resource for their clients.

My clients come to me because they need a new website or to refresh an existing website.

Keep in mind I keep the lines of coummication open while work on client projects. Mostly through email but also through multiple brief Skype meetings.

2. Website Types

I offer two types of websites.

  1. Github Pages – These are for small static informational websites that don’t change.
  2. Wordpress – For either small or large sites. These websites are for clients who want to easily create pages and posts. Like writing a word document or email.

3. Service Packages

I offer three service packages.

  1. Hourly - $60.00 an hourly – with a minimum of 2 hours. For large websites or projects that will take longer than a week. (Comes with 6 minor revisions)
  2. Build - $400.00 flat – For small to medium websites that are 5 pages or less. (Comes with 3 minor revisions)
  3. Maintenance - $100.00 flat - Returning clients only. Good for fixing minor issues.

4. Scope

This is where we talk about what I’m actually going to build and how long it will take. I prefer to write this out in detail. I want my clients to know in clear language what I am doing for them.

This is when my client picks their package, site type, layout, colors, fonts, and other design elements.

5. Contract

Once we have agreed to the specifics of what they want. I send my client a copy of my contract with the details of what I’m going to do for them. I ask them to review it, sign it, and send me a copy with their signature. Once the contract is signed we can move forward.

6. Resources

After the contract is signed, but before I begin building anything, my client provides me with the required resources.

  1. Written text copy for all pages
  2. Their business logo
  3. Media – images, audio, and/or video.

Note: all media must be owned by you, royalty free, and/or you have written permission from the owner to use it.

7. Development Build

Once the site is complete I send a link to my client to review my work. At this point my client can ask for minor revisions if they want to.

Major design or functional changes will require a new estimate. Once my client approves the development version we can move to the next step.

Note: Revisions depend on the service package you purchased.

8. Payment

Now that my client is happy with the development version of their site.

They send the full payment, as we agreed in the contract.

After the payment shows up in my account I proceed to the next step and transfer the web site to their host and the source files to them via Dropbox.

9. Transfer

My client gives me access to their hosting environment.

(Github pages or Wordpress host)

I then take the time to copy the site from my development server to their host and make the site live.

After the site is up and running I test it and troubleshoot any issues I find.

Once everything is working correctly I save all the source files to a zipped folder and send it to my client via Dropbox. I give my client up to 10 days to save the source files before I delete them. Either after my client lets me know they saved their files or after 10 days have passed, I delete the source files from my Drobox.

I do not have the storage space to store client source files long term.

10. Remove Access

Once the contract is complete I ask the client to either remove the temporary account they setup for me. Or, to change their password to remove my access from their host.

This improves their security by ensuring that only they have access to the admin section of their site from this point on.

Questions?

If you have any questions regarding this process feel free to email me. I'll be happy to answer your questions.

I usually go over all questions with my clients through Skype meetings.
