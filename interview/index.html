Front-End Developer Interview

General Questions

Q: Tell me about your Front-End Development work experience.
A: For the last 6 years I’ve worked as a professional Front-End Developer in team environments collaborating on large corporate web applications using Scrum / Agile methods.

I’ve designed wireframes, coded views in HTML5, CSS3, and Bootstrap 4. While using JavaScript and jQuery for functionality and validation.

After my last contract role ended I took a sabbatical to learn UX Design and to upgrade my JavaScript skills to include ES6/ES7.

Now I’m looking for a full-time role focused on Front-End Development.

Q: What did you do in your last role?
A: I was part of a three person UX Development team tasked with fixing code errors in front-end views for a Student Loan Repayment web application.

We compared the pages that were built by the offshore team against prototypes to find errors.

We rewrote Bootstrap, HTML, and SCSS code to correct the placement and spacing of components on pages. We tested our code visually and with software to make sure the views worked correctly on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.

After 5 months of fixing bugs the executive team moved creation of front-end views from offshore to our department. This reduced the time it took for design, coding, testing, bug fixing, and deployment

Q: Why did you leave your last position?
A: The contract ended.

Q: What have you been doing lately?
A: Job hunting while improving my skills with training, a few technical projects, and writing in my blog.

Q: Where did you learn your skills?
A: I learned from books, online video courses, and on the job in corporate environments.

Q: How do you keep updated on changes in the industry?
A: I use Feedly to follow a collection of Front-End Development related RSS feeds.

Q: Was your last job “contract” or “direct hire”?
A: My last few jobs were contract due to what was available at the time. I’d prefer direct hire but I’m open to contract-to-hire positions.
