Behavior Questions

1. Tell me about your work experience.

For the last 6 years I worked as a professional UI/UX Developer in team environments collaborating on large corporate web applications using Scrum / Agile methods.

I’ve designed wireframes, coded views in HTML5, CSS3, and Bootstrap 4. While using JavaScript and jQuery for functionality and validation.

I’ve also written documentation and contributed to style guides.

2. What did you do in your last role?

I was part of a three person UX Development team tasked with fixing code errors in front-end views for a Student Loan Repayment web application.

We compared the pages that were built by the offshore team against prototypes to find errors.

We rewrote Bootstrap, HTML, and SCSS code to correct the placement and spacing of components on pages. We tested our code, visually and with software, to make sure the components resized correctly on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.

After 5 months of fixing bugs the executive team moved creation of front-end views from offshore to our department. This reduced the time it took for design, coding, testing, bug fixing, and deployment.

3. Was your last job “contract” or “direct hire”?

My last few jobs were contract due to what was available at the time. I’d prefer direct hire but I’m open to contract-to-hire positions.

4. Why did you leave your last position?

The short-term contract for my job with Navient ended at the end of June in 2017. I wanted to be hired on full-time but it was only a short-term contract. I learned a lot and liked everyone I worked with. I’ve stayed in touch with many of them. Most have moved on to other positions outside Navient.

5. Why do you have a gap in your employment?

I started applying to UX Development positions immediately after my last contract ended. Most positions required a Bachelor’s Degree and 3+ years experience in React, Angular, and/or Vue. I was passed over because I lacked a degree or experience with those JavaScript frameworks.

But, I’m learning and improving my skills in those areas.

6. How did you spend your time while not working?

I used my time to train and improve my skills.

I designed and coded a Wordpress theme for my site. I recorded a YouTube series to demonstrate my skills: Wireframe to Wordpress. In Episode 9, I got creative and made a time-lapse of myself writing CSS code.

Later I used online video resources to improve my JavaScript ES6+ skills. Building a few small projects to learn and apply the new syntax.

When I could no longer afford my Wordpress hosting or domain. I rebuilt my blog using Hexo and hosted it on GitHub Pages for free.

I recently rebuilt an old dell desktop computer.

All while continuing to apply for jobs.

7. What are you looking for?

A full time job doing UX Development. I really enjoy writing CSS/SCSS code.

8. Where did you learn your skills?

I learned from books, online video courses, and on the job from other developers in team environments.

9. How do you keep updated on changes in the industry?

I use Feedly and follow a collection of technology related RSS feeds. I use Twitter and YouTube to follow developers who teach what they know.

10. Do you have any health or legal problems?

No health or legal issues. Other than minor blood sugar problems which makes me tired. I don’t eat sugary foods, consume caffeine, smoke, or drink alcohol. I’m only addicted to the internet, seltzer water, and burritos.

Most of the time I stay home, build linux-based computers, study to improve my skills, write in my blog, and watch YouTube videos. I haven’t had a speeding or parking ticket in 10 years.

While I was working at Navient I passed the 5C Security Clearance without issue. They people who reviewed my background check told me I was boring. Which I took as a compliment.

11. Do you have any interests?

UX Development, Linux, Raspberry Pi, Reading, Writing, Comic Books, Movies, TV Shows, and Retro Gaming.

12. What are your goals?

I want work for a great company while improving my skills. In my free time I want to write a novel. Someday I’d like to buy a house.

13. What does it mean to be professional?

Interpersonal skills, active listening to understand, problem solving, negotiation, emotional intelligence, compassion, acceptance, respect, time management, task prioritization, growth mindset, excellent written and verbal communication skills.

14. What motivates you?

I’m not competitive at all. I’m motivated to create, contribute, organize, and fix things. I’m a team player who enjoys collaborating. Seeing a project through from inception to completion is great.

My Questions

Tech Questions

  • Who are the stake holders?
  • Will I have one manager or many?
  • Do you have dedicated project managers?
  • What criteria is used to decide priority of tasks?
  • Do you use project management software like Trello?
  • Do you use a ticketing system to assign tasks?
  • Do you follow industry best practices?
  • How do you organize your CSS/SCSS code base?
  • Do you use git for revisioning and code deployment?
  • Does each developer maintain their own change log or is it automated?
  • Who writes the design briefs, style guides, and requirement documents?
  • If the end results are the same, can I use Linux Mint as my OS?

General Questions

  • How did you come to work for this company?
  • What do you love most about working for this company?
  • Who is the most successful recent hire and why are they successful?
  • Who didn’t succeed as a new hire and why?
  • What is the company’s biggest challenge this year and how will this role help overcome it?
  • How will I measure my performance to know I’m having a positive impact on this challenge?
  • What additional skills do you wish I had that would make me a better fit for this role?
  • What are the next steps?
