Process

Over the years I've developed a streamlined process to work with clients.

Before any agreements are made or contracts are signed I hold an informational meeting with a potiental client. This is where they explain their product idea and goals.

My goal during the first meeting is to figure out if I'm the right designer for their project. I'm also listening for indicators that they understand the services I offer and have the money to invest. This tells me they respect my time, experience, and skills.

Here's the questions I ask:

  • Am I right for your project?
  • Have you shopped around for other Designers?
  • Do you understand the services I offer?
  • Who is the target audience and what problem(s) are we solving for them?
  • What research and requirements gathering have you done?
  • What design deliverables do you want me to create?
  • What is your budget for this project?
  • What is the timeframe of the project?

Follow Up Steps

If I agree to take on a client we have a second meeting to hammer out the business aspects:

  • We review my contract together and clarify time frame, scope of services, mile stones, and pricing.
  • After the contract is signed and the first payment is made, then I get started.  

Pricing

I do not offer hourly based pricing.
Instead, I offer two pricing models for my services.

  • Project-Based Pricing
  • Value-Based Pricing

Project-Based Pricing

This model works best for small UX/UI projects or simple graphical assets. This is a flat fee for the design work I do on the project. It must be fully paid in advance before I start working. The total price is determined by the complexity, effort, and time it takes to create assets.

Value-Based Pricing

This model is best for medium to large projects. Each project is unique and so is it's value to you and your customers.

I start with the projected revenue this project will generate for your company and then estimate 30% of that. Then I add on the complexity, effort, and time it takes to create those assets.  

Payments

Value-Based project payments are split up into two parts. Half upfront and half upon completion of the project.

After full payment has been made. Then the asset files will be zipped and sent to the client.

Note: I do not store client project files after completion.

At the moment I only accept payments through: PayPal.

Corporate

I receive a lot of recruiter emails with job descriptions that don’t fit my career path. For clarification my focus is UX/UI Design.

Below is a “Reverse Job Description” I wrote as a counter-proposal to reflect the kind of role I’d be open to.

Reverse UX/UI Design Job Description

Position: UX/UI Designer
Location: 100% Remote (No requirements to visit the office or attend in-person events)
Duration: Full-Time (No contracts or agencies)
Interview: Phone and/or Virtual
Preferred Industries: Payment Processing, eCommerce, Renewable Energy, Technology Repair, or Recycling.
Annual Salary: $90K to $120K

Job Purpose:

User Experience (UX) Designer participates in customer interviews, surveys, and analytics to gather data on what users respond to.

This information is combined with a good understanding of business requirements and technical limitations. It is used in collaboration with stakeholders and management, to create project specifications and style guides.

User Interface (UI) Designer is responsible for translating project specifications and style guides into deliverables like flowcharts, site maps, user flows, wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes.

These deliverables are used by the development team as blueprints for building web and mobile applications.

Duties:

  • Translate concepts into user flows, wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes that provide intuitive user experiences.
  • Facilitate the client’s product vision by researching, conceiving, sketching, prototyping, and user-testing experiences for digital products.
  • Design and deliver wireframes, user stories, customer journey maps, personas, and mockups optimized for a wide range of devices and interfaces.
  • Identify design problems and devise elegant solutions.
  • Make strategic design and user-experience decisions related to core, and new, functions and features.
  • Take a user-centered design approach and rapidly test and iterate your designs.
  • Collaborate with other team members and stakeholders.
  • Ask smart questions, take risks, and champion new ideas.

Skills:

  • Expertise in standard UX software like Adobe XD, Figma, or Sketch.
  • Understands the limitations of HTML and CSS but is never required to write code.
  • Ability to work with clients to understand detailed requirements and design complete user experiences that meet client needs and vision.
  • Extensive experience in using UX Design best practices to design solutions, and a deep understanding of mobile-first and responsive design.
  • Ability to iterate designs and solutions efficiently and intelligently.
  • Ability to clearly and effectively communicate design processes, ideas, and solutions to teams and clients.
  • A clear understanding of the importance of user-centered design and design thinking.
  • Ability to work effectively in a team setting including synthesizing abstract ideas into concrete design drafts.
  • Be excited about collaborating and communicating closely with teams and other stakeholders in an agile environment.
  • Regularly deliver design solutions for review.

Qualifications:

  • 10 years of experience in corporate environments
  • User interface design
  • Experience with Agile project teams
  • Strong skills with Adobe XD and Figma
